Understanding Options Strategies

Each strategy has its own risk/reward profile and works best in different market conditions. Choose strategies that align with your market outlook and risk tolerance.

Basic Options Strategies
Covered Call
Overview

Sell call options against stock you already own to generate income.

When to Use
  • When you expect the stock to remain flat or rise slightly
  • When you want to generate income from existing holdings
  • When you're willing to limit upside potential for premium income

Wheel Strategy
Overview

A systematic income approach that cycles between selling cash-secured puts and covered calls.

When to Use
  • When you want consistent income generation
  • When you're willing to own the underlying stock long-term
  • In moderately bullish or neutral markets

Collar Strategy
Overview

Protect a stock position by buying a protective put and selling a covered call, creating a range with defined risk/reward.

When to Use
  • When you want to protect existing stock positions
  • During periods of heightened market uncertainty
  • When you're willing to cap upside in exchange for downside protection

Advanced Options Strategies
Put Credit Spread
Overview

Simultaneously sell a put option and buy a lower-strike put option, creating a bullish position with defined risk.

When to Use
  • When you have a bullish or neutral market outlook
  • When you want defined risk without owning the underlying stock
  • When implied volatility is relatively high

Iron Condor
Overview

A market-neutral strategy combining a bull put spread and a bear call spread, profiting when the stock stays within a range.

When to Use
  • When you expect the stock to remain within a defined range
  • During low volatility or sideways markets
  • When you want to profit from time decay with defined risk

Iron Butterfly
Overview

A market-neutral strategy similar to an iron condor but with tighter strikes, offering higher potential profits in a narrower range.

When to Use
  • When you expect very little price movement in the underlying
  • When you want to maximize theta decay
  • When you have a strong conviction about a specific price target
